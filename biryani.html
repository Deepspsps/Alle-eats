Biryani

90 Mins
X

Ingredients

1 cup basmati rice      1 and 3/4 cups water
1/4 cup curd                1/4 cup mint leaves
Coriander leaves        1 teaspoon lemon juice
1 tablespoon ghee      1 teaspoon ginger garlic paste
1 sliced onion               1 medium sized tomato
vegetables
(as per availability)      Spices as per taste
2 small bayleaf            one inch cinnamon
2 nos cloves
and cardamoms        1 teaspoon cumin seeds
1 small star anise

Directions

Step 1:


To a bowl add 1 cup basmati rice add water to it till immersing level. Soak it for 30 mins, set aside undisturbed. After 30 mins rinse well, drain water and set aside.

Step 2:

To a pressure cooker – heat 1 and 1/2 tablespoon oil, 1 and 1/2 tablespoon ghee – add one 2 small bayleaf, 1 inch cinnamon, 2 nos cloves, 1 no cardamom, 1 star anise, 1 green chilli slitted.

Step 3:

Let it splutter, give a quick mix. Then add 1 teaspoon cumin seeds let it crackle. Then add sliced onion, ginger garlic paste and required salt.

Step 4:

Saute until golden and transparent. Add 1 medium sized tomato roughly chopped. Saute until it is mushy and raw smell of tomatoes leave completely.

Step 5:

Chop all the vegetables available until they slightly shrink.

Step 6:

Now add 1/4 cup thick curd along with all the spices from turmeric and chilli powder to corander powder and garam masala.

Step 7:

After sauteing for 2 mins add the soaked basmati rice after washing for 2-3 times.

Step 8:

Close with pressure cooker lid with whistle put on. Pressure cook for 2 whistles in medium flame. Let pressure release by itself. Add 1 tablespoon coriander, leaves, 1 teaspoon lemon juice and 1 tablespoon ghee.

Ingredients

1 cup basmati rice      1 and 3/4 cups water
1/4 cup curd                1/4 cup mint leaves
Coriander leaves        1 teaspoon lemon juice
1 tablespoon ghee      1 teaspoon ginger garlic paste
1 sliced onion               1 medium sized tomato
600gm chicken           Spices as per taste
2 small bayleaf            one inch cinnamon
2 nos cloves
and cardamoms        1 teaspoon cumin seeds
1 small star anise


Directions

Step 1:


firstly soak saffron in water to prepare saffron water. Next, mix kewra drops in water and mix well to make kewra water.

Step 2:


Meanwhile, heat olive oil in a deep bottomed pan. Once the oil is hot enough, add cumin seeds, bay leaf, green cardamom, black cardamom, cloves in it, and saute for about a minute. Then, add chopped onion to it and saute until pink.

Step 3:


Now, add chicken into it with slit green chillies, turmeric, salt to taste, ginger-garlic paste, red chilli powder and green chilli paste. Mix well all the spices and cook for 2-3 minutes. Then, add hung curd into it and give a mix.

Step 4:


Turn the flame to medium again and add garam masala in it along with ginger julienned, coriander and mint leaves. Add kewra water, rose water and 1 tsp saffron water in it. Cook till the chicken is tender. Then add 1 cup cooked rice and spread evenly. Then add the remaining saffron water and pour ghee over it. You can now cook the dish without the lid or cover it with a lid to give a dum-effect due to the steam formation.

Step 5:


Cook for 15-20 minutes with a closed lid and garnish with 1 tbsp fried onions and coriander leaves. Serve hot chicken biryani with raita of your choice. Enjoy!
