Paneer

Overview


Paneer, a type of fresh cheese commonly used in South Asian cuisines, is made by curdling heated milk with an acidic ingredient like lemon juice or vinegar. While paneer is a dairy product, it contains a relatively high concentration of milk proteins, particularly casein and whey. These proteins can trigger allergic reactions in individuals sensitive or allergic to milk.


When someone with a milk protein allergy consumes paneer or dishes containing paneer, their immune system may identify these proteins as harmful substances. This recognition prompts the immune system to produce antibodies, particularly immunoglobulin E (IgE), as a defense mechanism. Upon subsequent exposure to paneer, these IgE antibodies trigger the release of histamine and other chemicals, leading to an allergic reaction.



Symptoms of a paneer allergy can vary in severity and may include:

1. Skin Reactions: Itching, hives, eczema, or swelling on the skin are common allergic responses after consuming paneer.

2. Digestive Issues: Gastrointestinal symptoms such as abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, or cramps might occur.

3. Respiratory Problems:Some individuals might experience coughing, wheezing, nasal congestion, or difficulty breathing as part of their allergic reaction.

4. Anaphylaxis: In severe cases, an allergic reaction to paneer can lead to anaphylaxis, a life-threatening condition characterized by a sudden drop in blood pressure, constriction of airways, and systemic shock. This requires immediate medical attention.


Cross-contamination during paneer production or the presence of milk proteins in other ingredients mixed with paneer can also contribute to allergic reactions.


In Conclusion

For those with known milk protein allergies or sensitivities, avoiding paneer and closely inspecting food labels for milk or dairy-derived ingredients is crucial. Substitutes made from non-dairy sources, such as tofu or plant-based cheeses, can be alternatives for individuals avoiding paneer due to allergies.
Seeking guidance from an allergist or healthcare professional is important for proper diagnosis, management, and the development of a safe dietary plan for individuals with paneer or milk protein allergies.
