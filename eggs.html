Eggs

Overview


Egg allergies occur when the immune system mistakenly identifies proteins in eggs as harmful substances. The two main proteins in eggs that often trigger allergic reactions are found in the egg whites: ovomucoid and ovalbumin, although other proteins can also be involved.


When someone with an egg allergy consumes eggs or egg-containing products, their immune system reacts by producing antibodies, specifically immunoglobulin E (IgE) antibodies, to combat these perceived threats. Upon subsequent exposure to egg proteins, these IgE antibodies signal immune cells to release histamine and other chemicals, causing allergic symptoms.



Symptoms of an egg allergy can vary in severity and might include:

1. Skin Reactions: Hives, eczema, itching, or swelling around the face, mouth, or other parts of the body.

2. Digestive Issues: Nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, diarrhea, or in severe cases, anaphylaxis characterized by a drop in blood pressure and potentially life-threatening symptoms.

3. Respiratory Problems:Nasal congestion, sneezing, coughing, wheezing, or difficulty breathing.


Egg allergies are more common in children, and many children outgrow them by the age of five. However, some individuals may continue to be allergic throughout their lives.

The proteins in eggs can be present in various foods, baked goods, and even in some vaccines, making avoidance challenging. Reading food labels diligently and being cautious when dining out or consuming processed foods is crucial for those with egg allergies.


In Conclusion

It's worth noting that while egg allergies can be challenging to manage, they differ from egg intolerance. Intolerance typically involves difficulty digesting eggs due to enzyme deficiencies, such as in the case of lactose intolerance with milk. Unlike allergies, intolerances do not involve the immune system.

Consulting an allergist or healthcare professional is essential for proper diagnosis and guidance on managing egg allergies. They can provide strategies for avoidance, potential desensitization options, and advice on handling allergic reactions if they occur.
