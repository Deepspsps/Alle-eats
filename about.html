Allergies are the 6th leading cause of chronic illness in the U.S. with an annual cost in excess of $18 billion. More than 50 million Americans suffer from allergies each year.


People can be allergic to some unusual things. These include sunlight, water, exercise-induced allergies, and even cold temperatures.


Allergies can occur at any age however, they tend to be more common in children. Allergies can also change throughout a person's life.


The development of allergies is influenced by both genetic predisposition and environmental factors, including early childhood exposure to potential allergens.



What are allergies?

Allergies are a common medical condition that occurs when the immune system reacts abnormally to a foreign substance, known as an allergen. Symptoms of allergies can range from mild to severe and can include sneezing, itching, hives, swelling, and difficulty breathing. Allergies can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, environmental factors, and lifestyle choices.The severity of allergies varies from person to person and can range from minor irritation to anaphylaxis — a potentially life-threatening emergency. While most allergies can't be cured, treatments and changes in diet can help relieve your allergy symptoms.


When to see a doctor?

You might see your health care provider if you have symptoms you think are caused by an allergy, and nonprescription allergy medications don't provide enough relief. If you have symptoms after starting a new medication, call the provider who prescribed it right away.
